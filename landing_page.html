The Problem

Bangkok's new BMA waste fee turns sorting into a direct financial decision — but the system that should produce sorting has never worked.

8,000 THB per m³ — Bangkok's BMA waste fee for large commercial buildings, effective October 2025. Every cubic metre of unsorted waste costs this.
0%
formal sorting in Bangkok's shared public bins — malls, airports, offices
8%
of Bangkok residents sort at home — the ceiling, not the floor
The gap isn't awareness. It's friction at the point of disposal — a person stands at a bin, guesses which slot is correct, and usually guesses wrong.
A medium mall generates ~50 m³/day. At 8,000 THB/m³ that's 400,000 THB/month. Diverting the ~25% recyclable fraction pays back Robin's cost in under 4 months.
The status quo
Manual sorting bins with poster instructions at Don Mueang Airport's office building — one of Robin's confirmed pilot sites.

Product Lineup

Two versions for two distinct needs — both built on the same AI core.

V6 — Autonomous
Drop & walk away.
Fully automatic. The user disposes normally and Robin handles the rest. Built for high-footfall environments where convenience is non-negotiable.
Target venues
  • ✓Shopping malls
  • ✓Airports & office buildings
  • ✓Hotels & large commercial spaces
V7 — Guided Disposal
Show it. Sort it.
Users present waste to a camera. The correct bin's lid opens automatically. Enforces piece-by-piece disposal and rejects contaminated items — building real awareness, not just automating the problem away.
Target venues
  • ✓Universities & schools
  • ✓BMA-operated public spaces
  • ✓Environmentally-focused organizations
